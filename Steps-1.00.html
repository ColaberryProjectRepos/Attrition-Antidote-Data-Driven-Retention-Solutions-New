Case Study
Executive Summary

In the face of substantial costs associated with employee turnover—up to 150% of an employee's annual salary—our project aimed to leverage data science to predict employee attrition accurately and reduce turnover. By integrating historical employee data into a predictive model, the project developed an interactive dashboard that empowers HR teams to identify attrition risks and implement proactive retention strategies effectively.


Background

High employee turnover rates pose significant financial and operational challenges to organizations. Traditional attrition prediction methods often fall short in accuracy and foresight, leading to ineffective retention strategies.


Problem Statement

Organizations require advanced tools to predict and mitigate employee attrition risks accurately, thus enhancing talent retention and organizational stability.


Objectives

To develop a sophisticated predictive model using employee data to identify potential leavers and enable HR departments to address attrition risks proactively.


Data Overview

  • Dataset Name: EmployeeAttritionDataset.xlsx
  • Data Size: 632 KB

Data Sources

Data was sourced from internal records and supplemented by external datasets available on platforms like Kaggle.


Data Collection Methods

Historical data was collected from HR databases, covering various metrics including performance ratings, tenure, satisfaction levels, and workload.


Data Features


Key features include:

  • Employee satisfaction
  • Performance ratings
  • Tenure
  • Workload

Methodology

  • Data Preparation: Utilized Python for data cleaning and preparation, ensuring robust model inputs.
  • Model Selection: Evaluated multiple models, with the SGDClassifier identified as the best performer.
  • Model Development: Developed on Microsoft Fabric, incorporating classification techniques such as logistic regression and random forests.

Results

  • Model Performance: Achieved an accuracy of 97.3%, precision of 98.5%, and recall of 96.0%.
  • Insights and Interpretations: Identified key predictors of turnover such as workload imbalance and job satisfaction.

Visualizations

Included interactive dashboards developed in Power BI, highlighting critical factors influencing employee departures.


Impact

The project significantly enhanced the HR team's ability to foresee and mitigate turnover, potentially saving large sums in associated costs.


Solution Impact

Implemented strategies have demonstrated potential savings by reducing turnover costs, with an ROI of up to 200% reported in targeted interventions.


Business Outcomes

  • Reduced Turnover: Demonstrated a potential reduction in turnover by 10 percentage points in high-risk departments.
  • Cost Savings: Estimated savings of $300,000 from retaining key staff.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Challenge: Incomplete data impacting model accuracy.
  • Solution: Applied advanced data imputation techniques to handle missing values effectively.

Lessons Learned

  • The critical importance of accurate data collection and preparation.
  • The need for continuous model tuning to adapt to changing workforce dynamics.

Future Directions

Exploring deeper AI-driven analytics for predictive insights and expanding the model to encompass more nuanced aspects of employee satisfaction and engagement.


References

Data sources from internal HR records and Kaggle datasets were crucial for developing the predictive model.
